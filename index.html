Zone 2 — Ex-core detectors (outside reactor vessel, in biological shield)
Startup range detectors
Proportional counters or high-sensitivity fission chambers; pulse-counting mode for very low flux near shutdown
~10⁻¹¹ → 10⁻² % FP
Intermediate range detectors
Compensated ion chambers (CIC); log-power and reactor period signals across 7–8 decades of power
~10⁻⁴ → 200% FP · period
Power range detectors
Uncompensated ion chambers; four symmetric channels providing axial flux difference and absolute power
~2 → 125% FP · axial shape
Ex-core fission chambers
Used for diversity and redundancy; operate across multiple decades; less sensitive to gamma than ion chambers
diversity · redundancy
⚡ Safety-hardwired path → Reactor Protection System (RPS) Period trip, high-flux trip, and power rate-of-change signals go directly to RPS via qualified hardwired channels. This path bypasses the digital twin entirely — no software latency in the safety loop.
Secondary path → Digital twin The same signals are mirrored to the edge layer and digital twin for predictive analytics, trend monitoring, and anomaly detection — but this path is non-safety-class and carries no control authority.

Closed-loop feedback: digital twin → robotic platforms

The twin continuously identifies regions with degrading sensor coverage, emerging anomalies, or high-uncertainty zones. It re-tasks the nearest available robotic asset (crawler, ROV, drone, or manipulator) to acquire targeted data, which is ingested back into the twin to reduce uncertainty. This closed-loop autonomy is the defining capability of the robotic monitoring system — inspection is no longer periodic and scheduled, but continuous and adaptive.
